<h3>Our Learning Approach</h3>
<p>The JSS Science teacher places inquiry at the heart of learning. It is
the central approach for teaching science in our classrooms. The curriculum
includes both learning about the methods of scientific inquiry and the
knowledge derived from those processes.</p>
<p>The learning goals that teachers strive to attain as a result of lesson
experiences are as follows:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>cultivating interest in learning science</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>enhancing mastery of subject matter</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>developing scientific reasoning</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>sharpening practical skills</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>understanding the complexity and ambiguity of experimental work</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>cultivating the ability to question critically for effective problem solving</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>developing teamwork abilities</p>
</li>
</ul>
<h4>Lower Secondary Science: Learning Through Collaborative Investigation</h4>
<p>In preparing our students for a VUCA world, the lower secondary science
curriculum at JSS seeks to develop in students a curious mind and to constantly
wonder about the physical and natural world. Part of the curriculum involves
students actively engaged in the learning of science through collaborative
science investigative tasks and learning about how science is relevant
to their daily lives.</p>
<p>Some of the features of the JSS Lower Secondary Science course include:</p>
<ul data-tight="true" class="tight">
<li>
<p>the use of demonstrations and inquiry-focused lesson materials to increase
student engagement in the classroom;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>investigative skills focusing on experimental planning;</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>collection and analysis of scientific data related to real-world scenarios
to make logical and justifiable conclusions.</p>
</li>
</ul>
